The lymphatic system

The lymphatic system is part of the immune system. It consists of a network of tissues and organs. Lymph fluid surrounds all cells in the body and is used to transport toxins, waste and other unwanted materials away from cells.

The lymphatic system does not have a pump that moves the lymph system around, like the heart pumping blood. Instead, lymph fluid is moved by your breathing and your movement.

The Vital Breathing exercise

This breathing exercise, called Vital Breathing, helps you to pump your lymph fluid by focusing on holding your breath. The stress your body experiences while you hold your breath acts as a pump for your lymphatic system.

Performing this exercise 3 times per day should cause noticeable improvements in your energy level and your body's ability to prevent and heal diseases.
